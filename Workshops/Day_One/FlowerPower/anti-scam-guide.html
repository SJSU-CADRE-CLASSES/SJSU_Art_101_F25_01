🌹 IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED IF YOU'VE BEEN SCAMMED

Time is critical! Follow these steps immediately to minimize damage and protect yourself.

🌹 Emergency Response Steps

1. Stop All Communication

Immediately stop talking to the scammer. Do not provide any more information, money, or personal details. Hang up the phone or close the conversation.

2. Document Everything

Write down all details: phone numbers, names, company names, what was said, amounts requested, and any other relevant information. Take screenshots if it was online.

3. Contact Your Bank/Credit Card

If you provided financial information or made payments, immediately contact your bank or credit card company. They can freeze accounts and reverse unauthorized charges.

4. Report to Authorities

File reports with the FTC (ftc.gov), FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (ic3.gov), and your local police department. Keep copies of all reports.

5. Monitor Your Accounts

Check your bank accounts, credit cards, and credit reports regularly for any suspicious activity. Set up fraud alerts with credit bureaus.

6. Change Passwords

If you provided login credentials, immediately change passwords for all affected accounts. Use strong, unique passwords for each account.

🔒 Prevention Strategies

Never Share Personal Information

Legitimate organizations will never ask for your Social Security number, bank account details, or passwords over the phone or email. If someone asks, it's likely a scam.

Verify Before Acting

If someone claims to be from a company or government agency, hang up and call the official number listed on their website or your statements. Don't trust caller ID.

Be Wary of Urgency

Scammers create false urgency to pressure you into making quick decisions. Take time to think and verify before acting. Legitimate organizations give you time to consider.

Check for Red Flags

Watch for: requests for gift cards or cryptocurrency, threats of legal action, promises of easy money, requests to keep secrets, or pressure to act immediately.

Use Call Blocking

Enable call blocking features on your phone, register for the National Do Not Call Registry, and consider using call screening apps to filter suspicious calls.

Educate Family Members

Share information about common scams with family, especially elderly relatives who are often targeted. Make sure everyone knows the warning signs and what to do.

⚠️ Common Scam Types to Watch For

IRS/Tax Scams: Fake tax collectors demanding immediate payment

Tech Support Scams: Fake computer problems requiring remote access

Romance Scams: Fake relationships leading to financial requests

Lottery Scams: Fake prizes requiring payment of fees

Grandparent Scams: Fake emergencies requiring immediate money

💡 Additional Safety Tips

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique, complex passwords for each account. Use a password manager to keep track of them securely. Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your computer, phone, and app software. These updates often include security patches that protect against new threats and vulnerabilities.

Be Social Media Savvy

Be careful about what you share online. Scammers use social media to gather information about potential targets. Review your privacy settings regularly.

Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong, it probably is. Don't ignore warning signs or let anyone pressure you into doing something you're uncomfortable with.
